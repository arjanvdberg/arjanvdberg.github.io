The Eurovision Song Contest with Eurovision Radio International - your music requests, comments and ideas: Eurovision Radio International never stops and is keen on keeping the music and news of the Eurovision Song Contest in the ears of the many fans that join the show each and every week via the usual means either on FM, Cable and also via PODCAST, Itunes, LISTEN AGAIN, live broadcast or Live Stream. Hightlights of the show

Interview with Polina Bogusevich (Winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2017, Russia):  The Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2017 took place in  the Olympic Palace of Tblisi, Georgia on Sunday, 26 November 2017 with the participation of 16 countries: Albania, Armenia, Australia, Belarus, Cyprus, Georgia, Ireland, Italy, Macedonia, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Russia, Serbia and the Ukraine. It was the 15th anniversary of the contest and as every year Radio International supports the Junior Eurovision Song Contest. The slogan for this year's event is "Shine Bright" and courtesy of radio colleagues Alain Forrotti and Ralph Hohn of a radio station in Berlin and Belgium, Radio International is extremely happy to be airing an interview with the Winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2017 Polina Bogusvich who represented Russia with the song "Wings"and gained a total of 188 points from televote and jury. Enjoy this interview on the show this week.
 
Eurovision Spotlight - Portugal's History in the Eurovision Song Contest:  Potugal for the very first time won the Eurovision Song Contest in 2017 in Kyiv, Ukraine last May with Salvador Sobral. As a result Portugal will be hosting Eurovision 2018 on May 08, 10 an 12.  It is a tradition that Radio International takes a look at the history of the the host country of the upcoming Eurovision Song Contest 2018. Our Eurovision experts in the UK and Ireland will in turn each talk about the Portugal's great Eurovision entries over the years.  Due to the emergency format of the show during October and November the Eurovision Spotlight was discontinued but returns this week and catches up with the History of Portugal at the Eurovision Song Contest and each of the correspondants will appear on the show this week to reflect on the songs that Portugal sent to the Eurovision Song Contest over the years. Listen to  Chris Poppe, Dermot Manning, Ross Bennett and Alasdair Rendall. It will be nice to hear some long forgotten classics again on the show this and next week. The new series will of course focus on the National Final Season to the Eurovision Song Contest 2018. 

Localitour d'Autore with Jalisse:  Jalisse represented Italy at the Eurovision Song Contest 1997 reaching Number 4 with "Fiumi di parole". Meanwhile Jalisse (Fabio and Alessandra) have been busy with many projects and new music plus supporting young talented artists as well as hosting their own radio show. Each week Radio International is delighted to broadcast the Song of the Week from the Italian Independed Music as selected by Jalisse and also broadcast on their weekly radio show. Eurovision Birthday File:  Adding something new to the show each and every week as from now is the Eurovision Birthday File brought to you by Sir David Mann from Studio 3 in London looking at the birthdays of Eurovision personalities over the past and coming days. Of course you will be hearing also the music of the Eurovision Birthday Kids.   
 
 
 
Eurovision Under Cover:  This ever so popular feature on Radio International is David Mann's weekly edition of the Eurovision Cover Versions where David Mann from Eurovision Norwich will be intoducing some of the finest Eurovision Song Contest entries covered by other singers. Just be surprised. You will be hearing the actual song that has been on the Eurovision stage followed by the cover. Listeners are encouraged to send in their cover versions they came across over the years to share with the other fans tuning into the show. Alternatively, listeners can send in their request for a particular Eurovision covered song using this link.

He also takes a look at some statistics such as viewing figures in the various countries and more. Nathan is website editor with escxtra.com.
