Live Interview with Federica Falzon (JESC 2014, Malta):  Following in the footsteps of Gaia Gauchi who won the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 for Malta in Kiev with the song "The Start" - is Federica Falzon. Having the same vocal teacher as Gaia - Gillian Attard, Federica is going very strong into the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2014 which this year is also hosted by last year's winning country - MALTA. The competition takes place in the Marsa Ship Yard inspired by Copenhagen as this year's Eurovision Song Contest also came from a ship yard. The date of the event is Saturday 15 November 2014. Federica will compete with the song "Diamonds". Just like last year when Gaia joined the Team of Radio International for an interview prior to the Junior Eurovision Song Contest, Radio International is extremely happy to welcome this year Federica to the show for a little chat. 

 
Live Interview with Vladislav Yakovlelv (Executive Supervisor of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest): The European Broadcasting Union's responsible man in all matters concerning the Junior Eurovision Song Contest - Mr. Vladislav Yakovlev which be joining this week's edition of Radio International for an interview regarding the Junior Eurovision Song Contest in general and in particular this year's edition which comes from Malta on Saturday 15 November 2014 when 16 countries will be competing for the Top 3 trophies. The countries are: Armenia, Belarus, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Georgia, Italy, Malta, Montenegro, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Slovenia, Sweden, The Netherlands and Ukraine. For Vladislav Yakovlev it is the second Junior Eurovision Song Contest he is responsible for from the side of the European Broadcasting Union (EBU) Find out details and additional information on the event during the interview with Vladislav Yakovlev. Luke Fisher has been employed by the EBU to run the website of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest and Luke, as a former Radio International Eurovision Newscaster will join JP in the studio this week as co-host. Also a Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2014 Special is in preparation for broadcast on 12 November 2014 hosted by JP and Luke.
 

Interview with Alexander Rybak (Eurovision Winner 2009, Norway):  Part of the guest line up of the OGAE Luxembourg Eurovision Gala on Saturday, 25 October 2014 were three winners of the Eurovision Song Contest. Loreen (Sweden 2012), Linda Martin (Ireland 1984 and winner in 1992) and Alexander Rybak (Norway 2009). Radio International was present and interviewed Alexander Rybak on his participation as composer at the national finals of the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 in Belarus and also in Malta and more. Alexander Rybak represented Norway and won the Eurovision Song Contest 2009 with the song "Fairytale". Tune in this week to get the latest from Alexander Rybak in the interview which will air on the show this week. 
 
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Austria in Eurovision:  With the start of a new quarter there is also a start with a new feature on Radio International which takes us right to the National Selections to the Eurovision Song Contest 2015. Austria initially won the Eurovision Song Contest in 1966 for the very first time with Udo Juergens singing "Merci Cherie" and then the second victory came in 2014 with Conchita Wurst and "Rise like a Phoenix" and with this will be hosting the 2015 edition of the Eurovision Song Contest. As in previous years the Eurovision Spotlight has reflected on the songs of the next host country and this year is no exception. The Radio International Eurovision Experts will be taking a look at Austria's history at the Eurovision Song Contest. The new series continues this week with Alasdair Rendall. Enjoy the flashback into Austria's entries to the Eurovision Song Contest.
