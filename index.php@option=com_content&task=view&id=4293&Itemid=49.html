Hightlights of the show

Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015: This week's edition of Radio International devotes most of it's content to the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 which was staged in Sofia's Arena Armeec in Bulgaria on 21 November 2015. 17 countries took part in the music competition for children including newcomers Australia and Ireland. The complete list of the 17 countries are: Albania, Armenia, Australia, Belarus, Bulgaria, FYRO Macedonia, Georgia, Ireland, Italy, Malta, Montenegro, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Slovenia, The Netherlands and Ukraine. This year's theme song of the event is "#Discover" sung by last year's Bulgarian representative Krisia. Consult the offical event's website Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 for all the latest news and the results concerning the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 and also you will also find all detailed information about the young artists. JP and David of Radio International were present at the event and conducted interviews with the young artists which you can hear during the upcoming shows and the video version of the interviews are available on the accompanying Eurovision Blog. This week on the show listen to interviews with the Top 5 countries of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 as well as the Producer of the show Gordon Bonello and the host Poli Genova.  The complete coverage of the Junior Eurovision Week 2015 can be viewed on our Eurovision Blog accompanying the website - CLICK HERE to see photos, watch the interviews, listen to the PODCAST of Radio International from Sofia and the video of the winning moments.

Interview with Shalisa (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, The Netherlands):  Following in the footsteps of the twins and then Julia last year in Malta Shalisa represented the Kingdom of the Netherlands at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Million Lights" receiving 35 points and with this ranking the Netherlands at Number 15 at the end of the voting. Shalisa and her team of dancers performed a nice love song and had a huge following in the hall as the Junior Eurovision Song Contest shows more popularity in the Netherlands than the adult version of the Eurovision Song Contest. Listen to the interview with Shalisa on this week's edition of Radio International.   You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here

Interview with Kamilla Ismailova (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, San Marino):  San Marino debuted at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 with Michele Perniola who then went on to represent San Marino at the Eurovision Song Contest 2015. At the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 San Marino had help from Russia as Russia's Kamilla Ismailova represented San Marino with the song "Mirror" receiving 36 points and with this ranking at Number 14 at the end of the voting. JP and David had the pleasure to meet Kamilla and her team during the rehearsal week in Sofia. Listen to this particular interview in this week's edition of Radio International. You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here

Interview with Chiara and Martina (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, Italy): Italy debuted at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2014 and also winning it with Vincenzo Cantiello and the song  "Tu primo grande amore" in Malta's Marsa Shipyard. In 2015, twin sisters Chiara and Martina represented Italy at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest with the song "Viva" reaching Number 16 with a total of 34 points. JP and David met the twins during the rehearsal days for an interview which you can hear on this week's edition of Radio International. You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here

Interview with Knez (Montenegro 2015): OGAE Portugal in collaboration with the Municipality of Setubal in Portugal held their annual Eurovision Live Concert 2015 in the Luisa Todi Forum on Setubal on 12 September 2015 with a great line up of artists from the national and international Eurovision music scene who stood on the stage of the Eurovision Song Contest or took part in the national finals. JP went to Setubal and had the pleasure to meet and chat with some of the singers. This week Radio International airs the final interview of this particlar event and it is with the amazing voice from the Balkans - Knez who represented Montenegro at the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Adio" penned by Zijelko Joksimovic who himself sang twice at the Eurovision Song Contest (2004 and 2012). Knez and "Adio" successfully qualified Montenegro to the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest and then ranked at Number 13 with 44 points. In Setubal, Knez performed his powerful Eurovision entry "Adio" but also some other material. Enjoy listening to this interview on this week's editon of Radio International. You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - The Best of Melodifestivalen:  With the victory of Mans Zelmerloew in Vienna winning the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Heroes" the Eurovision Song Contest 2016 will be stage in Stockholm's Globen Arena in Sweden next May. Without doubt Swedish Television produces the biggest National Final there is to select the song that represents Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest filling large halls across the country in the stages of Melodifestivalen - sometimes even bigger than the Eurovision Song Contest itself. In view of the upcoming event in Stockholm the team of Radio International looks back at some of the greatest songs that emerged from the Melodifestivalen over the years. The series continues this week with Dermot Manning who takes a look at Melodifestivalen 2004.

Just be surprised. You will be hearing the actual song that has been on the Eurovision stage followed by the cover.
