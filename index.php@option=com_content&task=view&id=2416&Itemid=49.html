The Big Fabrizio Faniello Quiz 2011 (Part 3 of 3):  Radio International in collaboration with CAPSOUND from Germany which is Fabrizio Faniello's record lable will be conducting the Fabrizio Faniello Quiz 2011. Fabrizio Faniello's album "No surrender" is featured in this quiz and you can win this album which is doing very well in many countries around the globe. In order to win this album courtesy of CAPSOUND you have to listen very carefully to the show where the quiz will be explained. The successful winner of the Quizz will be awarded with the FABRIZIO NO SURRENDER PACKAGE containing of: the Fabrizio signed album "No surrender", a poster, a key chain, the singles "Know me better" & "No surrender". So watch out - something you should not miss. Should you have missed the previous two weeks' parts of the quiz fear not - you can still catch up in listening to the show from the last two weeks and get your answer from there - for that click here.
All you have to do this week during the live airing is to listen to the third song Radio International will play and instantly email the name of the song together with the name of the two songs which were played in the previous two weeks. Good Luck!
 
Live Interview with Rikki Peebles (United Kingdom 1987): Radio International is delighted to welcome to this week's show the UK's entrant to the 1987 Eurovision Song Contest which took place in Brussel, Belgium - Rikki Peebles. "Only the light" was the song that represented the UK at the contest hosted by Victor Lazlo, reaching Number 13. Rikki will joins the studio team via phone from Scotland to chat about his experience at Eurovision and of course what has he been up to over the past years. Rikki Peebles is also known to have written songs for the 70s supergroup Middle of the Road and Marmalade.

Live Interview with Andy Brooks, organiser of the Home Composed Song Contest 2011: Radio International is delighted to announce the continuoued collaboration with the organisers of the Home Composed Song Contest 2011. Last year's event was won by the United Kingdom which like at the Eurovision Song Contest means that the winning country will virtually host the following year's contest. Andy Brooks is the prime organiser of the event and has launched the website which you can visit here and see the further details and may the best home composed song win. :)  For the history of the Home Composed Song Contest click here.  Andy will be joining the team in the studio for an interview to tell us indebt details about this particular contest.
 

Eurovision Song Contest News:  Luke Fisher joins the team in the studio every week to bring you the news and the latest developments connected to the Eurovision Song Contest.
