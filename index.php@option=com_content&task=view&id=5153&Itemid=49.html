   Experience the Eurovision Song Contest with Radio International

Radio International and Livestream TV with Chat Room: Radio International is aired on their Facebook channel live every Wednesday where fans can join and see how we actually do Radio International and listen to the show while chatting with the other Eurovision fans joining the community.  Enjoy and tell your friends to join and be part of the Eurovision Radio International Community! Also become part of the Eurovision Radio International Facebook Group which accompanies the show and is available here. It contains lots of Eurovision News from the major Eurovision News Websites and also announcements. 

Please note that many interviews we air are also available as video interview which can be viewed on our Radio International YouTube Channel. Just take a look and enjoy also see the artists talking to us. 
 
Hoorah!!! Christmas Week has arrived and the Big Days are near. As every year at this time Radio International sends the annual Eurovision Christmas Card to the listeners and fans of the Eurovision Song Contest. Enjoy this week's special Christmas themed show getting you into the right mood for the Festive Season. The team of Radio International invites you to the weekly Ultimative Eurovision Experience. Apart from the means of FM Radio in the Netherlands on FM 106.8 and the audio stream from the studio via www.radiointernational.tv, TUNEIN and on Digital Cable 916 in the Netherlands, the show will also be web-televised via our FACEBOOK GROUP PAGE from the studio and linking up the many international Eurovision fans and friends and play your requests and read your messages out on air. The channel includes a Chat Room. Tell your friends to join in - SPREAD THE NEWS. Normally, the simulcast is from 2000 – 2300 hours CET when Eurovision fans can join in and chat amongst each other via the Chat Room and request their favourite Eurovision entry. You can also use Twitter to be in touch with the studio during the live broadcast on Wednesday. The address is: @RadioInt 

The Eurovision Song Contest with Eurovision Radio International - your music requests, comments and ideas: Eurovision Radio International never stops and is keen on keeping the music and news of the Eurovision Song Contest in the ears of the many fans that join the show each and every week via the usual means either on FM, Cable and also via PODCAST, Itunes, LISTEN AGAIN, live broadcast or Live Stream. You can also find Radio International (RTV Katwijk) on TuneIn. Please feel free to submit your comments, ideas and requests of Eurovision songs and Radio International is delighted to play your requests during the shows. Click on CONTACT to submit your request to the programme. You can also reach us on Twitter: @RadioInt

Hightlights of the show

Interview with Nicole (Eurovision Winner 1982, Germany):   The annual Eurovision Convention of Eurovision Club Germany took place recently in Cologne's Gloria Theatre in Germany with the participation of three Eurovision performers of 2017:  Norma John (Finland 2017), Sunstroke Project (Moldova 2017) and Brendan Murray (Ireland 2017). In addition, two Eurovision Winners were part of the guest lineup. Anne Marie David who won the Eurovision Song Contest 1973 for Luxembourg, although being from France with the song "Tu te reconnaitras". The highlight of the show was Germany's first every Eurovision Winner from 1982 - Nicole - who with the song "Ein bisschen Frieden" won the Eurovision Song Contest in Harrowgate in Germany back in 1982, Radio International's JP had the big pleasure to meet all guests and conduct interviews with artists. Over the next weeks you will hear these interviews on Radio International starting in January 2018. Although one is kept for a special Christmas present to the fans and that is the interview with Nicole which will be broadcast on the show this week. The second hour of the show is primarily devoted to the Eurovision Winner and some of her music from the days back when... :) Enjoy this special.

Eurovision Spotlight - Portugal's History in the Eurovision Song Contest:  Portugal for the very first time won the Eurovision Song Contest in 2017 in Kyiv, Ukraine last May with Salvador Sobral. As a result Portugal will be hosting Eurovision 2018 on May 08, 10 an 12.  It is a tradition that Radio International takes a look at the history of the the host country of the upcoming Eurovision Song Contest 2018. Our Eurovision experts in the UK and Ireland will each talk about the Portugal's great Eurovision entries over the years. Listen to Dermot Manning and Alasdair Rendall closing out this particular series. It will be nice to hear some long forgotten Portuguese classics again on the show this and next week. The new series will of course focus on the National Final Season to the Eurovision Song Contest 2018. 

Localitour d'Autore with Jalisse:  Jalisse represented Italy at the Eurovision Song Contest 1997 reaching Number 4 with "Fiumi di parole". Meanwhile Jalisse (Fabio and Alessandra) have been busy with many projects and new music plus supporting young talented artists as well as hosting their own radio show. Each week Radio International is delighted to broadcast the Song of the Week from the Italian Independed Music as selected by Jalisse and also broadcast on their weekly radio show. Eurovision Birthday File:  Adding something new to the show each and every week as from now is the Eurovision Birthday File brought to you by Sir David Mann from Studio 3 in London looking at the birthdays of Eurovision personalities over the past and coming days. Of course you will be hearing also the music of the Eurovision Birthday Kids.   
 
 
 
Eurovision Under Cover:  This ever so popular feature on Radio International is David Mann's weekly edition of the Eurovision Cover Versions where David Mann from Eurovision Norwich will be intoducing some of the finest Eurovision Song Contest entries covered by other singers. Just be surprised. You will be hearing the actual song that has been on the Eurovision stage followed by the cover. Listeners are encouraged to send in their cover versions they came across over the years to share with the other fans tuning into the show. Alternatively, listeners can send in their request for a particular Eurovision covered song using this link.

Eurovision Song Contest News: Nathan Waddell joins the team in the studio every week to bring you the news and the latest developments connected to the Eurovision Song Contest such as the National Finals or if it is music releases of Eurovision performers, special concerts, gossip, etc Nathan will have it all for us during the weekly edition of Radio International. He also takes a look at some statistics such as viewing figures in the various countries and more. Nathan is website editor with escxtra.com. In addition, Eurovision News is available on www.eurovision.tv,
 
New Song Releases by Eurovision Artists:  Radio International is always keen to know how the careers of Eurovision performers develop after their performance at the event. The show is in collaboration with many Music Companies that supply Radio International with the latest songs of their signed up artists to bring the muisc closer to the fans. On a weekly basis Radio International receives songs which are being featured on the show.
