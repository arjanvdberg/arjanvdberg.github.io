(Hour 1 and Hour 2) The Craziest Eurovision Song Contest entries:  Most of the team members of Radio International are on holidays during the month of September. Therefore, JP is joined by Eric Lehmann in the studio to bring you three special editions of the show. Eric & JP will play and discuss about the craziest Eurovision Song Contest entries as sent in by the listeners to the show and also as compiled by the Team of Radio International.  This is in the first two hours of the show whereas the third hour as another special in store for you. Enjoy!

Interview with Malta's JAnvil (Malta National Selections to the Eurovision Song Contest:  Like last week where Radio International broadcast an interview with UK's Josh Dubowie this week Radio International is delighted to air an interview with one of Malta's Fine Singers - JAnvil who is a Multi-Talent from the Islands of Malta. He has been attempting various times to obtain the ticket represent Malta at the Eurovision Song Contest. In addition to this he shows in his musical style a lot of diversity. Some of his hits include "Choose your Number" and the great "Topsy Turphy" which includes the voice of Donna from Donna & Joe who represented Ireland at the Eurovision Song Contest 2005 with the song "Love". Radio International interviewed the singer during Malta's National Selection to the Eurovision Song Contest ealier on in 2012. 

(Hour 3) Eurovision meets South Africa (Fourth Series, Part 2):  After the success of the first three series' Radio International is delighted to present to you the next and fourth series. This is the second of three parts where Radio International broadcasts a series of shows where you can hear the enthusiasm of South African artists recording Eurovision Song Contest entries into Afrikaans. This truely is something very special and we wish to thank Roy van der Merve, Neil and Carel van Eden for contacting Radio International to make the Eurovision Fans aware of this ongoing project and the interest int he Eurovision Song Contest in South Africa.
