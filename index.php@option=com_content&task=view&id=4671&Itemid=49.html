Hightlights of the show

Interview with Dana International (Eurovision Winner 1998, Israel): At the recent Antwerp Pride in Belgium Radio International had the big pleasure to meet the Winner of the Eurovision Song Contest 1998 and probably the reason for the change of the Eurovision Song Contest's development to what it is now. JP and a bunch of other journalists had an interview session with Dana International in the City Hall of Antwerp in August. After which she performed a 30 minute concert open air to the fans including her winning Eurovision entry from the Eurovision Song Contest 1998 - "Diva". Dana International is also part of the line of up the OGAE Luxembourg Eurovision Galanight 2016 on 22 October 2016. For details there go to www.galanight.net.
 
Interview with Elisabeth Andreassen (Norway 1996, Norway 1985...):  Part of the Eurovision Winning Duo Bobbysocks is Elisabeth Andreassen who together with Hanne Krogh won the Eurovision Song Contest 1985 for Norway with the song "La det swinge". Before forming the Bobbysocks Elisabeth, also known as Bettan, represented Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest 1982 as part of the girl group Chips when "Dag efter Dag" came 8th. "Bettan" has a record as the woman with the most participations in the Eurovision Song Contest, tied with Lys Assia (Switzerland) and Sue of Peter, Sue & Marc (Switzerland). In total she has participated four times, three times in duets (with Kikki Danielsson in 1982 as Chips, Hanne Krogh in 1985 as Bobbysocks, and Jan Werner Danielsen in 1994 with the song "Duett" reaching Number 6) and once on her own when in 1996 Elisabeth became the Runner Up with the song "I evighet". She has participated various in Sweden's Melodifestivalen and that is also where Radio International met Elisabeth earlier in March at the Opening Reception of Melodifestivalen 2016 in Stockholm, Sweden. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here

The Eurovision Champion this week runs down the entries from Monaco to the Eurovision Song Contest in the order how the fans voted for the songs from Monacco.
