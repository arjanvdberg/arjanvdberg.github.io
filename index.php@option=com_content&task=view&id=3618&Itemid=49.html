THE NEXT FOUR EDITIONS OF RADIO INTERNATIONAL ARE DIFFERENT - Holidaying Radio International: After this week's show some members of the Team of Radio International will be on their well-earned summer vacation and with this the show content is also slightly different. JP and David Mann will be doing a bit of a recab of Eurovision 2014 with reports and interviews from Copenhagen, but also from some national finals. Watch out for interview with Chiara, Anne-Marie David, Linda Martin, Suzy, Gianluca Bezzina and his family, Twin Twin, Axel Hirsoux, Tanja, Katrina of Katrina & the Waves, Tom Dice and many many more which will be aired during the next four weeks on Radio International. Don't miss it at the usual time of 2000 - 2300 hours CET each and every Wednesday. The normal format of Radio International will return on Wednesday, 08 October 2014 with all the regular items on the show.  
 
Eurovision Song Contest 2014 - Radio International on location in Copenhagen - a recap:  The Team of Radio International while at the Eurovision Song Contest 2014 produced lots of material in terms of interviews with the Eurovision 2014 performers plus some specials which you will hear on Eurovision Radio International during the next weeks curing your Post Eurovision Depression as much as possible. A special thanks go to the team members on location working hard to get this coverage to you. A pile of photos and videos are on our Facebook and YouTube Channel. But here is the link to the Photo Albums where you can enjoy the photos from two weeks of Copenhagen and Eurovision Song Contest 2014. Album 1   |  Album 2    |   Album 3  |   Album 4. A detailed listing of the voting results of the two Semi Finals and Grand Final is available here.   
 
Interview with Sheila Furguson of the Three Degrees: A couple of years ago The Toma Fund of Newcastle in the United Kingdom organised an amazing event on Wednesday, 11 April 2012 in the Sage, Gateshead with the involvement of many Eurovision Winners and performers such as Linda Martin, Johnny Logan, Bobbysocks, Anne-Marie David, Scott Fitzgerald, Scooch, Josh Dubovi, Nicki French, Black Lace and others. The aim of the event was to raise money in aid of the the Toma Fund and children suffering from cancer. Details about the Toma Fund can be found here and who wishes to donate money for this good cause please see the website how to donate.  Radio International was on location as the only Eurovision media having the great pleasure to interview the artists which were already broadcast back in 2012, however, not of one very famous person that actually was the co-host of the event: Sheila Furguson of the Three Degrees. JP & Chris took the chance to chat with Sheila about her musical career and the link to the Eurovision Song Contest. The Three Degrees had many mega hits in the 70s such as "When will I see you again" and "Dirty ol' man" just to mention a few. Details about the group can be found here. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International. 
 
Interview with Linda Bengtzing (Melodifestivalen 2005, 2006, 2008 & 2011): Nathan Wadell from escXtra and Radio International had the big pleasure to meet and speak to one of the most energetic singers of Sweden who early last August was part of the guest line up for London's Eurofest - Ms. Linda Bengtzing. For several years Linda took part at Melodifestivalen which is Sweden's national selection process to find that song to represent Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest but to date could not manage to achieve that goal. Hopefully in the near future. Details about Linda can be found here but also Radio International would like to say thank you to escXtra for the interview with Linda which you can hear on this week's edition of Radio International.  
 

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Eurovision Songwriters - This week:  The Radio International Spotlight through the summer of 2014 features the songwriters at the Eurovision Song Contest and in this series the Eurovision Experts of Radio International will be taking a look at the songwriters that brought us many great Eurorivison entries. Ending the current series this week is Alasdair Rendall who will be taking a lock at the works of Sweden's Fredrik Kempe and the many nice songs he has added to the ears of the many Eurovision fans.   
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight XL - The Semi Final entries that should have qualified for the Grand Final:  Returning to the airwaves this week and that in a longer version than the usual slot is Marcus Keppel-Palmer who will be joining JP for the third hour of this week's edition of the show to take a look at some of the great Eurovision entries that actually should have qualified for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest. This week listen to the choice of JP who selected 20 songs he thinks should have qualified for the Grand Final. Marcus and JP chat about those entries and of course also play ten of them. Radio International has received some submissions from listeners and they will be featured in the weeks to come on the show, however, if you would like to submit your choice of Semi Final entries that should have made the Grand Final please send your Top Twenty list of songs to CONTACT and let us know why you select each song.   
 
 
Malta Summer Hit Song Contest 2014:  On 06 August 2014 edition of Radio International Deo Grech from Malta One Radio talked about the Malta Summer Hit Song Contest 2014 which is now in the preparation stages where the 18 competing songs are being introduced on Malta's F-Living TV channel. Courtesy of Marc Calleja-Bayliss from escflashmalta.com Radio International will introduce the songs on the radio every week. The Grand Final of the contest will be in September and by that time all songs will have been aired on Radio International. 
 

Eurovision UnderCover:  Last week, Radio International started a brand new series on the show featuring Eurovision Cover Versions where David Mann from Eurovision Norwich will be intoducing some of the finest Eurovision Song Contest entries covered by other singers. Just be surprised. You will be hearing the actual song that has been on the Eurovision stage followed by the cover. Enjoy the brand new series.  Listeners are encouraged to send in their cover versions they came across over the years to share with the other fans tuning into the show. Alternatively, listeners can send in their request for a particular Eurovision covered song using this link. David Mann's been digging deep in his treasure box for this week's Eurovision Cover song.
