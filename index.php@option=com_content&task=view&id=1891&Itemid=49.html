Eurovision Song Contest with Radio International and Oikotimes.
   
Pink Nation Exclusive 2010: On Friday, 30 April 2010 seven Eurovision nations took part in a special Eurovision Promotion  event called "Pink Nation Exclusive". The event was held in the AEC Antwerp, Belgium. Radio International had a team on location to conduct interviews with the Eurovision performers which you can hear in this week's edition of the show. For full details of the Pink Nation Exclusive event click here. And check out the photos while listening to the show by clicking here. Interviews with Eurovision performers during this week's show by:
  • Albania - Juliana Pasha - It's all about you
  • Armenia - Eva Rivas - Apricot Stone
  • Belgium - Tom Dice - Me and my guitar
  • Greece - Giorgios Alkaios - Opa
  • Ireland - Niamh Kavanagh - It's for you
  • Malta - Thea Garrett - My Dream 
A summary of the event with interviews (on video and photos) is available here
 

Eurovision Song Contest 2010 - Share the Moment - Profiles:  All 39 countries have selected their entry to the 55th Eurovision Song Contest which takes place on 25 & 27 May 2010 for  the Semi Finals and the Grand Final on 29 May 2010. Radio International's Alasdair Rendall takes a final look at the countries' entries profiles where you learn more about the performer and the song.  This week the series ends with the next lot of countries:   Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, Ukraine and United Kingdom. This way Radio International will bring you closer the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest entries so you are already a bit familiar with the songs when you see them in the Semi Finals and Final. You can also take a look at the videos of the songs available here. A new series with the Team will start on our 02 June 2010 edition of Radio International.
 
escflashmalta.com:  escflashmalta.com is a brand-new website to keep the Maltese Eurovision fans and population up-to-date with the happenings in the World of the Eurovision Song Contest in connection with national and international performers. Editor-in-Chief Marc Calleja-Bayliss founded the new website just recently with the end of escmalta.com. Good luck Marc with the new Maltese Eurovision News website and the new venture.
