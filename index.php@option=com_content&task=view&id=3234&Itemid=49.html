A Eurovision Summer with Radio International:  Eurovision Radio International does not stop throughout the summer time and is keen on keeping the music of the Eurovision Song Contest in the ears of the many fans that join the show each and every week via the usual means either via PODCAST, LISTEN AGAIN, live broadcast or Live Stream. Please feel free to submit your requests of Eurovision songs that remind you of summer and Radio International is delighted to play your requests during the shows in the summer. Click on CONTACT to submit your request to the programme. 
 
Interview with Krista Siegfrids (Finland 2013): A bundle of energy from Finland and a lot of fun is this year's Finnish entry to the Eurovision Song Contest - Krista Siegfrids who successfully qualified Finland for the Grand Final in Malmoe and then achieved Number 24 with the song "Marry me". Krista and her team have been out and about before and after the Eurovision Song Contest performing. W12P's Glen Bartlett met and interviewed the singer in London lately and we thank our colleagues from Oystermouth Radio in Wales, UK for sending us this interview for broadcast. You can hear this funny interview on this week's edition of Radio International. 
 
Interview with PeR (Latvia 2013):  Latvia was represented by PeR (Please Explain Rhytm) with a kind of Britpop sounding song called "Here we go" which kicked off Semi Final 2 of the Eurovision Song Contest 2013. Unfortunately, Latvia did not gain enough points in their Semi Final to qualify for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2013. Disappointingly the song only reached Number 17 in their Semi Final, however, they are a great bunch of Party Boys and rocked the Euroclub. Radio International previously aired an interview with PeR courtesy of David Mann when he met the singers as the London Pre-View party. Now find out more about the singers by listening to a second interview done in Malmo. You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - interview 1  |  interview 2  
 
Interview with Alexandra Pascalidou:  The two weeks to the run up of the Eurovision Song Contest always involves more then a houndred press conferences and there was also no exception to this in Malmoe. Swedish TV SVT engaged as Press Conference Host a former host of a Semi Final of Melodifestivalen. She was also the TV commentator for Greek TV commenting the Eurovision Song Contest 2005 - a lady with many talents originating from Greece and now living and working Sweden - Alexandra Pascalidou. Eric and JP chatted with Alessandra during a break of the Press Conferences in the Press Centre in Malmoe.  You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - The Favourite Non-Winning Eurovision entries per country:  The Eurovision Song Contest 2013 is over and all our correspondents have given us their views and favourite songs of this year's contest. So time to get into the summer 2013 with a brand new series in which our correspondents in the UK and Ireland look at our favourite non-winning Eurovision entries per participating country. This week Marcus Keppel-Palmer continues the series with the countries Serbia, Serbia & Montenegro, Slovakia and Slovenia.
 
Eurovision UnderCover:  Last week, Radio International started a brand new series on the show featuring Eurovision Cover Versions where David Mann from Eurovision Norwich will be intoducing some of the finest Eurovision Song Contest entries covered by other singers. Just be surprised. You will be hearing the actual song that has been on the Eurovision stage followed by the cover. Enjoy the brand new series.  Listeners are encouraged to send in their cover versions they came across over the years to share with the other fans tuning into the show. Alternatively, listeners can send in their request for a particular Eurovision covered song using this link. David Mann's been digging deep in his treasure box for this week's Eurovision Cover song. Don't miss it!
 

New Song Releases by Eurovision Artists:  Radio International is always keen to know how the careers of Eurovision performers develop after their performance at the event.
