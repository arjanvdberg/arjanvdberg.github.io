Show 14 April 2010
Listen to the Show:       Hour 1     |    Hour 2     |   Hour 3     |    Play List
 
Eurovision Song Contest with Radio International and Oikotimes.
 
Eurovision Interview with Vukasin Brajic (Bosnia-Herzegovina 2010): Radio International is delighted to announce the interview with Vukasin Brajic who represents Bosnia-Herzegovina at the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest with the song "Munja I Grom" (Thunder and Lightning). Vukasin is in Semi Final 1 as Song Number 8. His website you can find here.
 
Interview with Kelly Dixon from "Magic of Eurovision": On tour through the Netherlands on 23 dates from 08 April until 13 May 2010 is the ensemble that pays tribute to the Eurovision Song Contest. "The Magic of Eurovision" is a highly acclaimed smash hit show based on a cultural phenomenon. Kelly Dixon is the organiser of this fantastic roadshow which successfully toured in the United Kingdom and made it's way into the Netherlands. Hear and see the alltime Eurovision classics from Lulu, Cliff Richard, Sandie Shaw, ABBA, Celine Dion, Bucks Fizz and Alexander Rybak just to mention a few. Check out the website here for the list of dates in the Netherlands. Radio International is delighted to welcome Kelly Dixon for an interview of this nice show.  
 

Eurovision Interview with Gjoko Taneski (FYR Macedonia 2010): The Radio International News Man Luke Fisher met Gjoko Taneski in Belgrade earlier this week and had the chance of an interview with Gjoko. He represents the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia at the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest in Semi Final 1at Number 15 with the song "Jas ja imam silata" (I have the strength). This interview is courtesy of escdaily.com 
 
Eurovision Interview with Sirusho (Armenia 2008):  Sirusho was invited as special guest to the Malta Eurosong 2010 last February. Radio International had the big pleasure to yet again meet the fantastic singer from Armenia who with her entry "Qele Qele" reached a remarkable 4th place at the Eurovision Song Contest 2008 in Belgrade, Serbia. Radio International is happy to broadcast an interview Sirusho did with the team in Malta.  
 
Tribute to Teddy Scholten:  Teddy Scholten was the winner of the 5th Eurovision Song Contest back in 1959 with the song "Een Beetje" with which she won for the Netherlands. She died after a short illness on 08 April 2010 but the news was only released on Monday 12 April 2010. Radio International does a little tribute to the Winner of the 1959 Eurovision Song Contest. 
 
Eurovision Song Contest 2010 - Share the Moment - Profiles:  All 39 countries have selected their entry to the 55th Eurovision Song Contest which takes place on 25 & 27 May 2010 for  the Semi Finals and the Grand Final on 29 May 2010. In the second editon of the new series this week Radio International takes a weekly look at the countries' entries profiles where you learn more about the performer and the song.  Chris Poppe will be continuing with the next lot of countries:   Georgia, Germany, Greece, Iceland, Ireland, Israel and Latvia. This way Radio International will bring you closer the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest entries so you are already a bit familiar with the songs when you see them in the Semi Finals and Final. You can also take a look at the videos of the songs available here.
 
Radio International Eurovision 2010 Predictions:  Also starting this week each team member of Radio International  will each week be giving us their prediction which countries they think will be ending up in the Top 10 of the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest. Once the 2010 Eurovision Song  Contest Winner has been announced we can see whose prediction is closest. This week Chris Poppe's prediction for the Top 10 of the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest looks like this:  10th Cyprus, 9th Romania, 8th Belarus, 7th Greece, 6th Armenia, 5th Norway, 4th Germany, 3rd Azerbaijan, 2nd France, 1st Ireland. 
 
Eurovision in Concert 2010: Last week Radio International interviewed one of the prime organizers of this event - Rene Romkes. The event is to take place on 24 April 2010 near Zaandam north of Amsterdam. Rene talked to us about the organisation and the ever growing guest list. All the details are available here. However, due to the tragic news that overshadowed Poland this week with the death of the President of Poland, the Polish representative to the Eurovision Song Contest - Marcin Mrozinski cancelled his participation.

  Your 3 Minutes of Fame on Radio International:  This week we had Aiia Medinika from Latvia in the "hot seat"  who told us her first Eurovision moments and a little bit more.
