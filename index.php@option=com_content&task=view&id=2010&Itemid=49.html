Eurovision Song Contest with Radio International and on Oikotimes.TV

Eurovision Song Contest Interview - Didrik Solli-Tangen (Norway 2010):  At the end of July 2010 Lilian, JP and Eric were in Stockholm for a huge Eurovision Event where many Eurovision artists were interviewed. Radio International continues to air these interviews on the show this week.  Listen to an interview with Didrik Solli-Tangen who represented Norway at the 2010 Eurovision Song Contest with the song "My heart is yours" reaching Number 20 in the Final.

Euro Friend Song Contest 2010 - Qualifiers of HEAT 3:  Radio International in collaboration with the Euro Friends Song Contest 2011 will be announcing the next ten qualifiers of  HEAT 3.  Andy Simon, prime organiser of this contest will be joining us once again to reveal the songs which will go through into the next step of the competition. Check out what the international juries voted for via the Face Book Page of the contest. During the past couple of weeks fans of this contest could vote for their ten favourite songs within the third one hundred songs of the Eurovision Song Contest. Heat 4 will be starting real soon. Join to vote for your favourite Eurovision songs by clicking on this link which takes you to the Face Book Page of the Event. Remember, you have to be a member in order to join the voting. 

Euro-Mamaia Festival Interview with Olivier Vanhoutten:  EURO-MAMAIA is celebrating it's 40th Anniversary at the end of August 2010. Olivier Vanhoutten will be joining the team of Radio International to inform us all about the history of the Romanian event and also gives a rundown of which famous artists are going to be singing at EURO-MAMAIA. 

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - 30th Anniversary of the 1980 Eurovision Song Contest:  There is always a reason to celebrate and this week Alasdair Rendall joins us during the show to recap the special songs, scenes and parts of the 1980 Eurovision Song Contest that this year celebrates it's 30th Anniversary. Johnny Logan won in The Hague, The Netherlands for Ireland. But more find out when Alasdair Rendall joins us on this week's edition of Radio International.  

Eurovision Song Contest News:  Also Eurovision Newsreaders need to go on holidays which is the case for Luke Fisher who is away for a couple of weeks. Chris Poppe stands in for Luke this week to bring you the news and the latest developments connected to the Eurovision Song Contest. Whether it is music releases of Eurovision performers, special concerts, gossip, etc Chris will have it for us during the weekly edition of Radio International. In addition, Eurovision News is available on www.eurovision.tv, oikotimes.com, esctime.com, escdaily.com, esctoday.com and escflashmalta.com. 

  • Germany - Nadja from No Angels being in court regarding HIV case
  • United Kingdom - Frances Ruffelle, Nicki French and James Fox all appearing in shows
  • Sweden, Portugal, France - New album releases from Darin, Vania and Jessy Matador
  • United Kingdom - Margi Clarke wanting to represent the United Kingdom in 2011
  • Greece - Four names in the frame of Greece at the Eurovision Song Contest 2011
  • Norway - Dates for Melodi Grand Prix have been announced
  • Germany - Ireen Sheer gets married
  • Netherlands - Marga Bult (ESC 1987) celebrates 40th Stage Anniversary 21 Aug 2010 in Denekamp, The Netherlands
  • Sweden - Drifters added Swedish Version of Dutch 2010 Eurovision entry "Sha la li" to their album
This is just a selection. Go to www.oikotimes.com for detailed information.
