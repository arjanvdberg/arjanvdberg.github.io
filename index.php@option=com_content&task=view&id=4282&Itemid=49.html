Hightlights of the show

Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015: This week's edition of Radio International devotes most of it's content to the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 which was staged in Sofia's Arena Armeec in Bulgaria on 21 November 2015. 17 countries took part in the music competition for children including newcomers Australia and Ireland. The complete list of the 17 countries are: Albania, Armenia, Australia, Belarus, Bulgaria, FYRO Macedonia, Georgia, Ireland, Italy, Malta, Montenegro, Russia, San Marino, Serbia, Slovenia, The Netherlands and Ukraine. This year's theme song of the event is "#Discover" sung by last year's Bulgarian representative Krisia. Consult the offical event's website Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 for all the latest news and the results concerning the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 and also you will also find all detailed information about the young artists. JP and David of Radio International were present at the event and conducted interviews with the young artists which you can hear during the upcoming shows and the video version of the interviews are available on the accompanying Eurovision Blog. This week on the show listen to interviews with the Top 5 countries of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 as well as the Producer of the show Gordon Bonello and the host Poli Genova.  The complete coverage of the Junior Eurovision Week 2015 can be viewed on our Eurovision Blog accompanying the website - CLICK HERE to see photos, watch the interviews, listen to the PODCAST of Radio International from Sofia and the video of the winning moments.

 

Interview with Poli Genova (Host of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015):  A very energetic Poli Genova hosted the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 leading through the event. Poli is no stranger to the Eurovision world as she represented Bulgaria at the Eurovision Song Contest 2011 in Duesseldorf, Germany with the song "Na inat" which came 12th in her Semi Final. JP from Radio International talked to Poli Genova right after the Press Conference for a little flashback to her Eurovision participation as well as how she got selected as host of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015. Listen to this interview on this week's edition of Radio International.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Vincenzo Caniello (Winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2014, Italy):   In 2014 Italy debuted at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest and also won it with Vincenzo Cantiello and the song "Tu primo grande amore". As the winner of the previous Junior Eurovision Song Contest, Vincenzo came to Sofia to hand over the trophy to Destiny as the Winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, but also he read the votes for Italy. During the Junior Eurovision week JP and David met the young singer at the Welcome Reception and also an interview about the development of his career since his victory in Malta's Marsa Shipyard last year. For the very first time Vincenzo tried this interview in English without translator. Enjoy listening to this charming interview.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Lena Stamenkovic (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, Serbia):  A tiny lady with a great voice Lena Stamenkovic represented Serbia at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Lenina pesma" (Lena's Song) receiving 79 points thus ranking at Number 7 at the end of the voting. Her performance reminded very much on the performance of the winning song in 2012 in Amsterdam when Anastasia Petryk won the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2012 for Ukraine with the song "Nebo". Listen to the interview with Lena on this week's edition of the show. You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Bella Paige (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, Australia): Like in the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 when Australia debuted at the Eurovision Song Contest with Guy Sebastian and "Tonight again" ranking 5th - Australia also debuted at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with an amazing 13 year old singer called Bella Paige performing for Australia "My Girls" receiving 64 points and thus ranking at Number 8 at the end of the voting. Australia's Superstar Delta Goodrem is behind the song and Bella also has European roots to Macedonia. But find out more about Bella during a short interview JP and David did after her Press Conference in Sofia.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Anna Trincher (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015,  Ukraine): After last year's harmonizing entry from Sympho-Nick, Ukraine's representative at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 was Anna Trincher who received 38 points and thus ranking the Ukraine at Number 11 at the end of the voting. If it is at the Eurovision Song Contest of at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest the Ukraine has always nice ideas how to stage their entries at the contests. This year it was a flower opening up and inside the great voice Anna Trincher performed the song "Pochny z sebe - Start with Yourself". Listen to the interview with Anna on this week's edition of Radio International.You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Live Interview with Sophia Patsalidis (Junior Eurovision 2014, Cyrpus) and ESCNEWS' Samuel Seakin: Samuel and escnews.org are part of a charity called Bloodwise and it involves last year's Junior Eurovision Song Contest participant from Cyprus Sophia Patsalidis as she sings the song to the charity. Sophia represented Cyprus in Malta's Shipyard at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2014 with the song "I pio omorfi mera - The most beautiful day" and came 9th in the final ranking of the contest. For full details listen back to the interview or follow the links below. The ESC News Group Christmas singles, '#TogetherAsOne', are available from 05 December 2015 is available at escnews.org/tao. To support the Sound of Vienna campaign for the same charity, visit escnews.org/flag and for the official website of the Bloodwise's Charity please visit: bloodwise.org.uk
 
Interiew with The Virus (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015, Georgia):  As every year Georgia sends in an act from the stables of the Bzikebistudio and this year was no exception as the Georgian representatives to the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 were The Virus performing the song "Gabede" (Dare) which received a total of 51 points and with this ranking at Number 10 at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015. So far Georgia won the Junior Eurovision Song Contest twice namely in 2008 when the Wasps Bzikebi won with "Bzzzzz" and then in 2011 when Candy won with "Candy Music".  JP and David met the group and were greated and farewelled with  huge hug by all the singers at once. :). Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International. Due to lack of time on the show this interview is postponed to next week's edition Radio International. We apologize for the inconvenience.
 
Interview with SINPLUS (Switzerland 2012): Also part of the massive guest lineup at the Eurovision Galanight 2015 organised by OGAE Luxembourg were the brothers Gabriel and Iwan collectively known as SINPLUS who represented Switzerland at the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 in Baku, Azerbaijan. Their entry "Unbreakable", unfortunately, only reached Number 11 in the Semi Final failing to qualify Switzerland for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 however a fantastic performance of the SINPLUS. How has their career been shaping up since 2012. JP chatted with the singers about their time at and after Eurovision and their upcoming future music projects. Listen to the interview during this week's edition of the show.  Due to lack of time on the show this interview is postponed to next week's edition Radio International. We apologize for the inconvenience.


Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - The Best of Melodifestivalen:  With the victory of Mans Zelmerloew in Vienna winning the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Heroes" the Eurovision Song Contest 2016 will be stage in Stockholm's Globen Arena in Sweden next May. Without doubt Swedish Television produces the biggest National Final there is to select the song that represents Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest filling large halls across the country in the stages of Melodifestivalen - sometimes even bigger than the Eurovision Song Contest itself. In view of the upcoming event in Stockholm the team of Radio International looks back at some of the greatest songs that emerged from the Melodifestivalen over the years. The series continues this week with Alasdair Rendall who takes a look at Melodifestivalen 2005.
