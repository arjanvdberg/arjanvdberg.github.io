Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013: Habemus Winner. The 11th edition of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest was staged at Palace of Ukraine in Kiev, Ukraine on 30 November 2013 with a very first victory for the sunny islands of Malta with Gaia Gauchi winning the competition in a thrilling voting with the song "The Start" followed by Ukraine's Sofia Tarasova and the song "We are one" and then third placed entry was Belarus' Ilya Volkov and the song "Poy so mnoy".
 
The Final Result as displayed on the Scoreboard in the Palace of Ukraine:
 
1.  130 Malta
2.  121 Ukraine
3.  108 Belarus
4.  106 Russia
5.  091 Georgia
6.  069 Armenia
7.  066 Azerbaijan
8.  059 The Netherlands
9.  046 Sweden
10. 042 San Marino
11. 041 Moldova
12. 019 FYR Macedonia
 
All in all the Team of Radio International  on location had the pleasure to do 22 interviews with the participants, artists, Eurovision Winners, directors, etc. Watch out for them to come on the weekly edition of Radio International.
 
Live Interview with Gaia Gauchi - The Junior Eurovision Song Contest Winner 2013 for Malta:  What an amazing result for the sunny islands of Malta with the very first victory at any Eurovision event. Members of the Team of Radio International were in Kiev and witnessed the whole thing. Sadly, every good event comes to an end, however, one still can remember the nice moments and for 11 year old Gaia a dream came true - To win the Junior Eurovision Song Contest. Radio International is proud to announce that Gaia will be joining us for a few minutes on the show for a few minutes to chat with us about her experience, the week in Kiev and the welcome home in Malta. Gaia Gauchi won the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 with the song "The Start" and a total of 130 points. Listeners and Viewers are encouraged to send us their questions to the Queen of Junior Eurovision 2013 during the show either via the chat on livestream or via CONTACT

Interview with Sofia Tarasova (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Ukraine): In 2012, Ukraine won the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2012 with Anastasiya Petrykand the song "Nebo". Sofia Tarasova almost followed the footsteps of last year's JESC winner as the became the Runner Up with the song "We are one" receiving 121 points for the Ukraine. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International.


 
Interview with Ilya Volkov (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Belarus): Two times winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest - Belarus sent in Ilya Volkov to represent his country with the song "Poy so mnoy" which translates to "Sing with me". He already took part as dancer in the Belarussian entry of 2012. Ilya came third in Kiev. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International.


 
Interview with Dayana Kirillova (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Russia): Russia was represented at this year's Junior Eurovision Song Contest by Dayana Kirillova and her team on stage caming 4th in the contest with the song "Mechtay" which translates to "Dream on". Listen to the interview on this week's edition of Radio International.
 
 
 
Interview with The Smile Shop (Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Georgia): Also already won twice the Junior Eurovision Song Contest is Georgia in 2008 and 2011. This year the entry came from The Smile Shop representing Georgia with the song "Give me your smile" reaching Number 5 in the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013. JP had the pleasureto meet the happy funny bunch from Georgia for an interview you can hear on this week's show. 


 
Interview with Victoria Romanova - Deputy Director of National Television Ukraine (NTU): The Junior Eurovision Song Contest as well as the Eurovision Song Contest require a lot of planning, organisation and coordination which is a joint effort by the Host Broadcaster and the European Broadcasting Union - The EBU. JP of Radio International spoke to Victoria Romanova literally 90 minutes before the live broadcast of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest about the organisation of this big pan-european music event and its popularity in the Eastern countries. Very interesting interview not to be missed. 
 
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History:  A new quarter has begun which also marks the start of a new series called Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History since Denmark for the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and with this will be hosting the 2014 edition of the contest in Copenhagen the Team of Radio International thought to continue the tradition to reflect on all the Danish Eurovision entries over the years. Denmark has won the Eurovision Song Contest three time. It was first in 1963 when Grethe and Jorgen Ingmann performed "Dansevise", then the Olson Brothers won in 2000 with the song "Fly on the wings of love" and then in 2013 with Emmelie de Forest and "Only Teardrops". This week Chris Poppe continuous the series looking back at the Eurovision entries from Denmark.
