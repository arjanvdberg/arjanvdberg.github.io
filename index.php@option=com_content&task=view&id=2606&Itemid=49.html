Melodifestivalen 2012 - Sweden's selection to the Eurovision Song Contest 2012:  Melodifestivalen 2012 is Sweden's biggest music event of the year where the Swedish nation select their most popular song which subsequently is used as the countries entry to the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 in Baku in May. More than 3000 songs were submitted which were narrowed down to a Top 32 competing in a number of qualification Heats, four Semi Finals and a Second Chance Round. Last Saturday night in the big Melodiffestivalen Final for 2012 Sweden's public and an international juries voted for Loreen to represent Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest in Baku with the song "Euphoria" which received a record number of televotes ever in the history of the contest (670551 televotes making it 32.7% of the voting public). The legendary Globen Arena in Stockholm which was the home of the Eurovision Song Contest 2000 where Denmark's Olson Brothers won with "Fly in the wings of Love" was the venue where the ten finalist competed for the ticket to Baku. The Team of Radio International and escflashmalta.com were on location and did many interviews you can hear in this week's edition of Radio International. See JP's Photo Collection from the Melodifestivalen 2012 - here and enjoy!

Interview with Helena Paparizou (Eurovision Winner Greece 2005): The Swedish-born Greek singer won the Eurovision Song Contest 2005 in Kiev, Ukraine for Greece with the song "My Number One". Helena Paparizou was the Interval Act of this year's Melodifestivalen while all the votes were counted in Stockholm. The singer performed a fantastic jazzy version of last year's entry from Sweden to the Eurovision Song Contest by Eric Saade called  "Popular". Eric and JP met the singer at the Aftershow Party for an interview.
