Hightlights of the show

Interview with Sestre (Slovenia 2002):  Radio International's JP had the pleasure in meeting Slovenia's representative at the Eurovision Song Contest 2002 - the lovely "Sisters" from Slovenia called SESTRE who reached Number 13 in the Grand Final of Eurovision 2002 with the song "Samo ljubezen". Sestre are Tomaž (Marlenna), Damjan (Emperatrizz) and Srečko (Daphne) had been performing together since 2000 under the name Suspender Sisters their act was very popular and the Sisters were invited to many Eurovision fan events such as OGAE Germany and OGAE Luxembourg where JP met them. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of the show. 
 
OGAE Germany's Annual Convention 2018 - Interview with Frank Albers:  OGAE Germany's Annual Convention is scheduled to take place on Saturday 20 January 2018 in Wirtshaus zum Isartal, Munich, Germany with the participation of Eurovision Winners from 1994 Paul Harington and Charlie McGhettigan (Ireland), Nathan Trent (Austria 2017), Omar Naber (Slovenia 2005 and 2017), Manuela Bravo (Portugal 1979) and surprise guests. Frank Albers - the clubs PR Manager will be talking to Radio International live during the show to tell us all the details of this year's edition of the OGAE Germany Convention. Tribute to France Gall (Eurovision Winner 1965, Luxembourg): Very sad news hit the fans of the Eurovision Song Contest early last week when it was announced that France Gall had passed away following a long suffering from cancer. France represented Luxembourg at the Eurovision Song Contest 1965 and became the Winner with the song "Poupe de Cire, Poupe de son". She also had a major chart success in 1988 right across Europe entitled "Elle Ella l'a". David Mann looks back at the singer's life and music in an extensive Tribute to France Gall on Radio International his week. Rest in Peace - France Gall.
The initially announced interview with Sestre who represented Slovenia at the Eurovision Song Contest 2002 is postponed till next week due to the Tribute to France Gall.

Interview with Nicole (Eurovision Winner 1982, Germany):   The annual Eurovision Convention of Eurovision Club Germany took place recently in Cologne's Gloria Theatre in Germany with the participation of three Eurovision performers of 2017:  Norma John (Finland 2017), Sunstroke Project (Moldova 2017) and Brendan Murray (Ireland 2017). In addition, two Eurovision Winners were part of the guest lineup. Anne Marie David who won the Eurovision Song Contest 1973 for Luxembourg, although being from France with the song "Tu te reconnaitras". The highlight of the show was Germany's first every Eurovision Winner from 1982 - Nicole - who with the song "Ein bisschen Frieden" won the Eurovision Song Contest in Harrowgate in Germany back in 1982, Radio International's JP had the big pleasure to meet all guests and conduct interviews with artists. Over the next weeks you will hear these interviews on Radio International starting in January 2018. Although one is kept for a special Christmas present to the fans and that is the interview with Nicole which will be broadcast on the show this week. The second hour of the show is primarily devoted to the Eurovision Winner and some of her music from the days back when... :) Enjoy this special.

Eurovision Spotlight - Portugal's History in the Eurovision Song Contest:  Portugal for the very first time won the Eurovision Song Contest in 2017 in Kyiv, Ukraine last May with Salvador Sobral. As a result Portugal will be hosting Eurovision 2018 on May 08, 10 an 12.  It is a tradition that Radio International takes a look at the history of the the host country of the upcoming Eurovision Song Contest 2018. Our Eurovision experts in the UK and Ireland will each talk about the Portugal's great Eurovision entries over the years. Listen to Dermot Manning and Alasdair Rendall closing out this particular series. It will be nice to hear some long forgotten Portuguese classics again on the show this and next week. The new series will of course focus on the National Final Season to the Eurovision Song Contest 2018.
