Eurovision Song Contest 2014 - Radio International on location in Copenhagen - a recap:  The Team of Radio International while at the Eurovision Song Contest 2014 produced lots of material in terms of interviews with the Eurovision 2014 performers plus some specials which you will hear on Eurovision Radio International during the next weeks curing your Post Eurovision Depression as much as possible. A special thanks go to the team members on location working hard to get this coverage to you. A pile of photos and videos are on our Facebook and YouTube Channel. But here is the link to the Photo Albums where you can enjoy the photos from two weeks of Copenhagen and Eurovision Song Contest 2014. Album 1   |  Album 2    |   Album 3  |   Album 4. A detailed listing of the voting results of the two Semi Finals and Grand Final is available here.   
 
Interview with Darja Svaiger (Slovenia 1995 & 1999):  The summer month is always a good chance to catch up with the many interviews the team of Radio International do throughout the year and this week there is a little nugget which is going to be aired on the show. It is an interview with Darja Svaiger who represented the little country of Slovenia twice. First it was back in 1995 when Darja performed "Prisluhni mi" which came 7th for Slovenia and then again four years on when in 1999 she returned for Slovenia with the song "The Light of Thousand Years" which reached Number 10 at the Eurovision Song Contest. Eurovision Club Germany invited Darja for a recent Fan Club Convention where JP met up with the singer for an interview in her dressing room of the Gloria Theatre in Cologne. Listen to this interview on this week's edition of Radio International. You can watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Eurovision Songwriters - This week:  Ralph Siegel & Bernd Meinunger:   The Eurovision Song Contest 2014 is over and after Eurovision is before Eurovision. Many countries will have Eurovision events throughout the summer into the autumn to bridge the Post Eurovision Depression. Also Radio International does not stand still and delivers in the weekly three hour show lots of items to make you forget the PED. In the current series the Eurovision Experts of Radio International take a look at the songwriters in Eurovision Song Contest. Continuing this week is Marcus Keppel-Palmer with taking a lock at the works of Ralph Siegel & Bernd Meinunger and the nice songs they have added to the ears of the many Eurovision fans including Germany's first victory in 1982 by Nicole "Ein bisschen Frieden". 
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight XL - The Semi Final entries that should have qualified for the Grand Final:  Returning to the airwaves this week and that in a longer version than the usual slot is Marcus Keppel-Palmer who will be joining JP for the third hour of this week's edition of the show to take a look at some of the great Eurovision entries that actually should have qualified for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest. Due to the success of this feature and listeners request Joseph Thornburg from Southern California, United States of America selected 20 songs he thinks should have qualified for the Grand Final. Marcus and JP chat about those entries and of course also play ten of them. Radio International has received some submissions from listeners and they will be featured in the weeks to come on the show, however, if you would like to submit your choice of Semi Final entries that should have made the Grand Final please send your Top Twenty list of songs to CONTACT and let us know why you select each song.

Eurovision Song Contest News:  Nathan Waddell took over the newscasting from Luke and joins the team in the studio every week to bring you the news and the latest developments connected to the Eurovision Songesc-n Contest.
