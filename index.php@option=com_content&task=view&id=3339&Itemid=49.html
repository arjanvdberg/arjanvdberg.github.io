Interview with Valentina Monetta (San Marino 2012, 2013, 2014):  San Marino is rather new to the Eurovision Song Contest starting to take part in 2008. Valentina Monetta is produced by Germany's Ralph Siegel who for the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 wrote "The Social Network Song" and in 2013 together with Mauro Bellestri the fantastic "Crisalide (Vola)" that to date is San Marino's best result at the Eurovision Song Contest reaching Number 11 in the Semi Final 2 in Malmoe. Valentina, Ralph and Mauro are preparing the participation for a third time for 2014. The San Marinese singer has invited for a Press Conference in London last weekend (16 November 2013) and rowing Radio International reporter Sir David "Colin Berry" Mann went to London to interview the San Marinese singer which you can hear on this week's edition of Radio International.

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight XL - The Semi Final entries that should have qualified for the Grand Final:  Returning to the airwaves this week and that in a longer version than the usual slot is Marcus Keppel-Palmer who will be joining JP for the entire third hour of this week's edition of the show to take a look at some of the great Eurovision entries that actually should have qualified for the Grand Finalof the Eurovision Song Contest. Due to the success of his feature and listeners request Robert Wahlstroem from Gothenburg in Sweden selected ten songs he thinks should have qualified for the Grand Final. Marcus and JP chat about those entries and of course also play'em. :) Radio International has received already some submissions from listeners and they will be featured in the weeks to come on the show, however, if you would like to submit your choice of Semi Final entries that should have made the Grand Final please send your Top Ten list of songs to CONTACT and let us know why you select each song.
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History:  A new quarter has begun which also marks the start of a new series called Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History since Denmark for the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and with this will be hosting the 2014 edition of the contest in Copenhagen the Team of Radio International thought to continue the tradition to reflect on all the Danish Eurovision entries over the years. Denmark has won the Eurovision Song Contest three time. It was first in 1963 when Grethe and Jorgen Ingmann performed "Dansevise", then the Olson Brothers won in 2000 with the song "Fly on the wings of love" and then in 2013 with Emmelie de Forest and "Only Teardrops". This week Marcus Keppel-Palmer continuous the series looking back at the Eurovision entries from Denmark. 
 
Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013:  The 11th and 2013 edition of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest will be staged at Palace of Ukraine in Kiev, Ukraine on 30 November 2013 after last year's victory of Anastasiya Petryk who won for the Ukraine with the song "Nebo". 12 countries have confirmed their participation of young artists from: Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Belgium, Georgia, Macedonia, Malta, Moldova, Netherlands, Russia, San Marino, Sweden and Ukraine competing for the trophy of the event. Consult the offical event's website Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 for all the latest news concerning the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and also you will also find all detailed information about the young artists. Members of the Team of Radio International will be present at the event and conduct interviews with the young artists which you can hear during the shows around the 04 December 2013. To get us into the mood for the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2013 Radio International welcomes JESC music requests from the listeners and viewers. CONTACT us for that.
