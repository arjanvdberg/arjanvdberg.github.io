Interview with Hera Bjoerk (Iceland 2010):  OGAE Portugal in collaboration with the Municipality of Setubal in Portugal held their annual Eurovision Live Concert 2015 in the Luisa Todi Forum on Setubal on 12 September 2015 with a great line up of artists from the national and international Eurovision music scene who stood on the stage of the Eurovision Song Contest or took part in the national finals. JP went to Setubal and had the pleasure to meet and chat with some of the singers. This week we start with Hera Bjoerk who in 2010 represented Iceland at the Eurovision Song Contest witht he song "Je ne sais quoi" coming 19th in Oslo. The year before she tried her luck in the Danish National Final with the song "Someday" which became the runner up to Brinck who in 2009 represented Denmark at the Eurovision Song Contest in Moscow where Hera was backing vocalist for Yohanna on "Is it true" which came 2nd to Alexander Rybak's Fairytake from Norway. JP caught up with Hera on her time in Southern America and upcoming events in her career. The interview was done in the Luisa Todo Forum of Setubal right after the show. Enjoy listening to this interview on this week's editon of Radio International. Due to lack of time the interview is postponed for broadcast to a later date. Sorry for the inconvenience.
 
Interview with Kurt Calleja(Malta 2012):  Another well-established artist that took part at the Eurovision Live Concert 2015 in Setubal, Portugal last September was Malta's Kurt Calleja who was Malta's Ambassador at the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 in Baku. He successfully qualified Malta for the Grand Final and then ranked at Number 21with his song "This is the night". Kurt was invited to Portugal to perform his Eurovision entry plus more songs from his repertoire. JP chatted with Kurt during the sound check. LIsten to this interview in this week's edition of Radio International.   You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with the Twiins (Slovakia 2011):  Also invited to the Eurovision Live Concert 2015 were the Twiins from Slovakia who represented their country at the Eurovision Song Contest 2011 with the song "I'm still alive" that unfortunately failed to qualify Slovakia to the Grand Final. The song ranked at Number 13 in Semi Final 2 in Duesseldorf. Nevertheless, Veronika and Daniela were great Ambassadors for their country and achieved the best result for Slovakia since the country returned to the Eurovision Song Contest. At the aftershow part of Eurovision Live Concert 2015 JP chatted with the Twiins. Listen to the interview on this week's edition of the show. Due to lack of time the interview is postponed for broadcast to a later date. Sorry for the inconvenience.
 
Interview with Marija Sestic (Bosnia & Herzegovina 2007): One of the nicest Balkan Ballads presented at the Eurovision Song Contest could be heard again at the Eurovision Live Concert 2015 in Setubal, Portugal performed live by Marija Sestic who represented Bosnia & Herzegovina at the Eurovision Song Contest 2007 in Helsinki with the song "Rijeka bez imena" coming 11th in the Grand Final. JP had the pleasure to speak with Marija after her soundcheck in the Luisa Todi Forum. Enjoy listening to the interview and also hear the song again.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight: Debut countries at Eurovision:  The summer has arrived and that also means the start of a new series on Radio International linked to a Eurovision theme. The Radio International Eurovision Experts put their brains together and came up with the great idea to reflext on the countries that debuted during the 60 years of the history of the Eurovision Song Contest. The series ends this week with Ross Bennett who will take a final look at the debut entries and this week it is Switzerland, Turkey, Ukraine, United Kingdom and Yugoslavia.
