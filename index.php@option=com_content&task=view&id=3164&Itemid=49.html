Welcome Reception & Red Carpet Interviews:  Every year at the Eurovision Song Contest the host city organises a Welcome Reception and Opening Ceremony which can be watched by the fans and local people and the many journalists and reporters covering the Eurovision Song Contest for their media. There are clips from the Red Carpet event where JP and Philip from eurovisiOradio had the pleasure to co-interview some of the singers of this year's Eurovision Song Contest. Throughout the upcoming editions of Radio International the clips will be injected into the show for your listening pleasure.  This week listen to an interview with Malta's Gianluca Bezzina.
 
Interview with Farid Mammadov (Azerbaijan 2013):  Azerbaijan almost won the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 with the amazing Farid Mammadov who represented Azerbaijan with the song "Hold me" reaching Number 2 with a totoal of 234 points. The Team of Radio International had the pleasure to meet him for two interviews. This is the second interview which is aired in this week's edition of the show.  You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Amadine Bourgeois (France 2013):  Following in the footsteps of Anggun to represent France at the Eurovision Song Contest 2013, Amadine Bourgois was the Ambassador of France directly qualified for the Grand Final as France is one of the Big 5 countries of Eurovision. This year's offering is called "L'enfer et moi" and reached Number 23 in Malmoe. Listen to a short interview with the French singer courtesy of Samantha Ross from escinsight.com.  You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with El Sueno de Morfeo (ESDM)(Spain 2013):  As Spain is also one of the Big Five Eurovision countries and with this directly qualified to the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest. El Sueno de Morfeo represented Spain this year with the song "Contigo Hasta el Final (With you all the way)" and unfortunately only ending up at Number 25 in the Grand Final, although they are big artists in their home country. JP and Eric met the singers for a couple of interviews which you can hear on this week's edition of the show. You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Natalia Kelly (Austria 2013): Earlier on this year Austria decided to hold a great national final which Natalia Kelly won and with this was selected to represent Austria at the Eurovision Song Contest 2013  with the song "Shine" and with this following in the footsteps of the Trackshittaz from 2012. Unfortunately, like in 2012 Austria could not qualify for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 as Natalia Kelly only could reach Number 14 in Semi Final 1 when only the Top Ten advance into the Grand Final. Lilian, David, Bert and JP met Natalia during her Meet & Greet with the fans and the media and conduction an interview which airs during this week's edition of Radio International. You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Glen Vella (Malta 2011):  One of the good side effects at a Eurovision Song Contest is the return of previous Eurovision participants as there are many parties around town where these artists then pop up and perform their Eurovision entry plus more. This was the case this year with the Bobbysocks, Linda Martin and Glen Vella entertaining the many international Eurovision Fans tha came to the Euro Fan Cafe.  The artists already arrived for the Welcome Reception and JP met Glen Vella at the Welcome Reception and used the chance of a quick update interview and his impression of being back in the World of Eurovision but not needing to compete. Glen represented Malta at the Eurovision Song Contest 2011 with the song "One Life" which failed by just one point to qualify for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2011. Listen to this interview on this week's edition of Radio International.
 
Live Interview with author Nikki Allen:  A huge fan of the Eurovision Song Contest herself and attending Europe's favourite TV Show for many many times Nikki Allen's ambition for a number of years has it to write and publish a book. This dream came true this week when her Eurovision novel was published. The novel is called "Somewhere in Europe" and tells a fantastic ficticious story around the Eurovision Song Contest on 532 pages. Nikki tells us some of the details on this week's edition of Radio International and moreover  you can buy this books via AMAZON just by following this link - Nikke Allen - Somewhere in Europe and enjoy!

New Song Releases by Eurovision Artists:  Radio International is always keen to know how the careers of Eurovision performers develop after their performance at the event.
