The Eurovision Song Contest 2014 is behind us and now it is time to cure that PED (Post Eurovision Depression) with Eurovision Radio International - your music requests, comments and ideas:  Eurovision Radio International never stops and is keen on keeping the music of the Eurovision Song Contest in the ears of the many fans that join the show each and every week via the usual means either via PODCAST, LISTEN AGAIN, live broadcast or Live Stream. Please feel free to submit your comments, ideas and requests of Eurovision songs and Radio International is delighted to play your requests during the shows. Click on CONTACT to submit your request to the programme. Radio International- the Ultimate Eurovision Experience, is extremely happy to now also being aired on Malta's Galaxy 105 Network every Monday lunchtime from 1300 - 1600 hours. 
 
Eurovision Song Contest 2014 - Radio International on location in Copenhagen:  The Team is back home from Copenhagen, Denmark which was the host city of the Eurovision Song Contest 2014. In its bag - lots of material in terms of interviews with the Eurovision 2014 performers plus some specials which you will hear on Eurovision Radio International during the next weeks curing your Post Eurovision Depression as much as possible. A special thanks go to the team members on location working hard to get this coverage to you. A pile of photos and videos are on our Facebook and YouTube Channel. But here is the link to the Photo Albums where you can enjoy the photos from two weeks of Copenhagen and Eurovision Song Contest 2014. Album 1   |  Album 2    |   Album 3  |   Album 4. A detailed listing of the voting results of the two Semi Finals and Grand Final is available here.

Interview with the Winner of the Eurovision Song Contest 2014 - Conchita Wurst (Austria 2014):  Prior the Eurovision Song Contest 2014, Radio International already aired a couple of interviews with the beared lady from Austria done by Debbie & David in Amsterdam and London at the Eurovision preview events. JP had the pleasure and chance to conduct an interview with Conchita Wurst in Copenhagen after her Press Conference with the international media. One of the most controversial entries in the Eurovision Song Contest 2014 is the entry from Austria's Conchita Wurst singing the Shirley Basseyish James Bond sounding theme "Rise like a Phoenix" which received a total of 290 points from during the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2014. In 2012, Conchita already tried her luck in the national Final of Austria to get the ticket to represent Austria in Baku. But she came only second and the ticket went to the Trackshittaz instead. David Mann also had a few words with Conchita after the Semi Final 2 Winners'Press Conference. Hear it all on this week's edition of Radio International.  You can watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Ovi and Paula Seling (Romania 2014 and 2010):  In 2010 Romania was represented at the Eurovision Song Contest by Ovi and Paula Seling with the fantastic entry that took them to Number 3 in the Grand Final. It was with "Playing with Fire". Both hosted the National Final in 2011 which was won by Hotel FM with the song "Change". Both continued with their own projects but in 2014 they got back together again to give it another attempt at the Eurovision Song Contest. This year is was with "It's a miracle" which reached Number 12 after successfully qualifying Romania into the Grand Final out of Semi Final 2. The Team of Radio International on location had the pleasure to meet and interview Paula & Ovi in their hotel in Copenhagen. Hear it on this week's edition of Radio Internaltional.   You can watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Eurovision 2014 - The Assessment:  The Eurovision Song Contest 2014 is over and after Eurovision is before Eurovision. Many countries will have Eurovision events throughout the summer into the autumn to bridge the Post Eurovision Depression. BUt also Radio International does not stand still and delivers in the weekly three hour show lots of items to make you forget the PED. In the new series the Eurovision Experts of Radio International will be taking a look at the 2014 Eurovision Song Contest starting this week with Marcus Keppel-Palmer assessing the Eurovision Song Contest 2014. 
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight XL - The Semi Final entries that should have qualified for the Grand Final:  Returning to the airwaves this week and that in a longer version than the usual slot is Marcus Keppel-Palmer who will be joining JP for the third hour of this week's edition of the show to take a look at some of the great Eurovision entries that actually should have qualified for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest. Due to the success of this feature and listeners request Matthias Breitinger from Germany selected 20 songs she thinks should have qualified for the Grand Final. Marcus and JP chat about those entries and of course also play ten of them. Radio International has received some submissions from listeners and they will be featured in the weeks to come on the show, however, if you would like to submit your choice of Semi Final entries that should have made the Grand Final please send your Top Twenty list of songs to CONTACT and let us know why you select each song.
