Interview with Sonia (United Kingdom 1993): OGAE Finland's annual Eurovision Cruise took place on 05 September 2015 on board of the Baltic Queen crossing from Helsinki to Tallin, Estonia. Way over 2000 international Eurovision fans on board celebrated a great time together with the invited artists Sanna Nielsen (Sweden 2014), Sonia (United Kingdom 1993) and Ivi Adamou (Cyprus 2012). Lilian, Roger and JP from Radio International had the pleasure to meet the artists after their performance for a quick chat. This week listen to the interview with Sonia who in 1993 represented the United Kingdom at the Eurovision Song Contest with the song "Better the devil you know" which came second to Ireland's Niamh Kavanagh who won the Eurovision Song Contest 1993 for Ireland with the song "In your eyes". Sonia also had a string of big hits in the 90s mainly produced by the Hitfactory of Stock, Aitkin and Waterman. Enjoy this interview on this week's edition of the show.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Interview with Ivi Adamou (Cyprus 2012):  Also guest at the Eurovision Cruise 2015 organised by OGAE Finland was Ivi Adamou  who represented Cyprus at the Eurovision Song Contest 2012 in Baku with the song "La La Love" which came 15th. Of course Ivi also performed her Eurovision entry as part of the Mini Concert on the Baltic Queen after which Lilan, Roger and JP from Radio International also had the pleasure to meet and interview the young singer on her time in Baku and her career development after the Eurovision Song Contest 2012. Enjoy listening to this interview on this week's edition of the show.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here
 
Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015:  Europe and Australia is fast approaching the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 which will be staged in Sofia, Bulgaria on 22 November 2015 from the Arena Armeec where so far 17 countries have confirmed participation in the music competition for children including newcomers Australia and Ireland. Editor in Chief of Eurovision Ireland Garrett Mulhall will be joining Radio International on a weekly basis to update the listeners on the progress in the Irish selection process for the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 as well as the overall JESC progress. A former Eurovision Winner as well as two times participant at the Eurovision Song Contest is involved - Niamh Kavanagh. The official website of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 is available here or details are also available on Wikipedia.   
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - The Best of Melodifestivalen:  With the victory of Mans Zelmerloew in Vienna winning the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 with the song "Heroes" the Eurovision Song Contest 2016 will be stage in Stockholm's Globen Arena in Sweden next May. Without doubt Swedish Television produces the biggest National Final there is to select the song that represents Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest filling large halls across the country in the stages of Melodifestivalen - sometimes even bigger than the Eurovision Song Contest itself. In view of the upcoming event in Stockholm the team of Radio International looks back at some of the greatest songs that emerged from the Melodifestivalen over the years. The series continues this week with Dermot Manning who takes a look at Melodifestivalen 2001.
