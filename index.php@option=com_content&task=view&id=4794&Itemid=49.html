Hightlights of the show

Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 - The Recap (Part 4): This week the story of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 closes as the Team of Radio International was in Malta - the Home of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016. The 14th edition of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest was in Mediterranean Conference Centre (MCC) on  20 November 2016, 1600 hours CET with the participation of 17 countries: Albania, Armenia, Australia, Belarus, Bulgaria, Cyprus, FYRO Macedonia, Georgia, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Malta, Poland, Russia, Serbia, The Netherlands and Ukraine. The show was hosted by Valerie Vella and Bed Camille and they had the honours to also announce the Winner of the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 being Mariam Mamadashvili from Georgia with the song "Mzeo". It is also the third time that Georgia won the contest. The previous two times when Georgia won theJunior Eurovision where in 2008 and 2011. This year's theme song of the event is "#Embrace" sung by last year's Junior Eurovision Song Contest Winner for Malta - Destiny. Consult the official event's website Junior Eurovision Song Contest for all the latest news concerning the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 and also you will also find all detailed information about the young artists. Members of the Team of Radio International were at the event to interview the young artists which you can see as video interviews on the accompanying Eurovision Blog. 

Interview with Poli Genova (Bulgaria 2011 and 2016): The best ever placed result for Bulgaria at the Eurovision Song Contest as Poli Genova with the song "If love was a crime" came fourth at the Eurovision Song Contest 2016 with a total of 307 points. Poli is no stranger to the fan base of the Eurovision Song Contest since she represented Bulgaria already once before in 2011 with the song "Na inat" which unfortunately at that time did not qualify Bulgaria for the Grand Final of the Eurovision Song Contest and reached Number 12 in the Semi Final 2. However, in 2016 Poli achieved the best ever result for Bulgaria at the Eurovision Song Contest reaching Number 4 with "If love was a crime". Further Poli also hosted the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2015 last December from Sofia, Bulgaria and was also on the guest list at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 in Malta being the Interval Act. Radio International's JP caught up with the singer during the rehearals. Listen to this interview on this week's edition of Radio International. You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here.  
 
Interview with Lidia Ganeva (Junior Eurovision 2016, Bulgaria, Number 9):  Bulgaria hosted the Eurovision Song Contest 2015 from Sofia in an amazing way and great spirit which was won by Destiny from Malta. Poli Genova was not the only Bulgarian visiting the Junior Eurovision 2016 in Malta but also the very young singer Lidia Ganeva who represented Bulgaria at the contest with the song "Magical Day" coming 9th. JP had the pleasure to meet the tiny lady with a great voice for a little chat. Listen to this interview on this week's edition of Radio International.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here.  
 
Interview with Klesta (Junior Eurovision 2016, Albania, Number 13): Another little lady with an amazing voice represented Albania at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016. It was Klesta performing the song "Besoy" which ranked 13th at the end of the voting sequence on 20 November 2016. Klesta fully enjoyed all of this media experience and performing in front of such a large audience.  Enjoy this interview with the young lady on Radio International this week.  You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here.
 
Interview with Shir and Tim (Junior Eurovision 2016, Israel, Number 15):  It was Israel's second particiaption at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest with Kids.il were the debutants back in 2012 in Amsterdam  and this year Shir and Tim represented Israel at the Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2016 with the song "Follow my heart" which ranking Israel at Number 15 at the end of the voting sequence on the night. Before that at the Israeli Press Conference JP had the pleasure of an interview with the young singers which you can hear on this week's edition of Radio International.    You can also watch this interview on our YouTube Channel - click here.  
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Ukraine as Host of the Eurovision Song Contest 2005:  Last week we finished the mini series on Radio International as our Eurovision correspondents took a look at the Eurovision entries from Ukraine since Ukraine will be hosting the Eurovision Song Contest 2017 following  Jamala's victory in Stockholm at the 61st edition of the contest with the song "1944". Ukraine initially won the Eurovision Song Contest in 2004 after their second attempt as Ukraine only joined the Eurovision family back in 2003. The Winning Song of Ukrain's first victory was "Wild Dances" performed by Ruslana when the Eurovision Song Contest 2004 came from Istanbul, Turkey. Just recently, it was decided that the Eurovision Song Contest 2017 will be hosted once again by Ukraine's capital city Kiev on May 09 & 11 for the Semi Final 1 and 2 and the Grand Final to take place on May 13. In 2005 Kiev also hosted the Eurovision Song Contest and Alasdair Rendall of Radio International will be taking a look at some of the highlights in that Eurovision Song Contest.
