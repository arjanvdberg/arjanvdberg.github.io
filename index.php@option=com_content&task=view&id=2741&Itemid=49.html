Interview with Lys Assia (Switzerland 1956, 1957 & 1968): The Mother of the Eurovision Song Contest as Lys Assia is the very first Winner when it all kicked off in the Teatro Kursal in Lugo Switzerland back in 1956 when seven countries competed for the very first trophy. Each country performed two songs. Lys Assia became the Winner for Switzerland with herthen French language entry called "Refrain". It is now known where her second entry "Das alte Karussel"  ended up in the very first Eurovision Song Contest. She then represented Switzerland in the following two years in 1957 with "L'enfant Que J'etais" which reached Number 8 and in 1958 Lys Assia came second with "Giorgio". In 2012 she attempted again to represent her native Switzerland at the Eurovision Song Contest with a Ralph Siegel composition called "C'etait ma vie" which came 8th in the Swiss National Selection for the Eurovision Song Contest 2012. Lys Assia as the "Mother" of the Eurovision Song Contest likes to be with her "Family" and is very often a guest of honour during the Grand Final. Also the 2012 event was no exception and just before the Loreen's Winner's Press Conference JP met Lys Assia and did an interview with her which you can hear during this week's edition of Radio International. 

Interview with Gaitana (Ukraine 2012): The host of the 2005 edition of the Eurovision Song Contest - Ukraine - was represented by bubbly and energetic Gaitana with the song "Be my guest" which successfully went from the Semi Final into the Grand Final and ther it ended up at Number 16. Lilian, Roger and JP had the pleasure to meet Gaitana in the Baku Press Centre right after her Press Conference and conducted an interview with the Ukrainian artist. You can hear this interview during this week's edition of Radio International but in addition also she already has released a follow-on single onto the music market which Radio International will also feature on the show this week.
