Interview with Roberto Bellarosa (Belgium 2013):  Eurovison Live Concert 2013 took place for the fifth time at the usual location in the open-air Auditorium Jose Alfonso in Setubal, Portugal with the participation of many Portuguese artists and international Eurovision performers such as Vania Fernandes, Daniela Varela from Flor-de-Lis, Birgitta Haukdal, Vlad Mirita of Nico & Vlad, Alex Panayi, Buket Bengisu, Jalisse, Moran Mazor, Chen Aharoni and last but certainly not least Belgium's representatives at the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 coming 12th with the song "Love kills" with Finnish co-composition - Roberto Bellarosa. Obviously, Roberto made a very good impression  to the Eurovision fans since he is invited by many OGAEs and events throughout Europe such as Eurovision Live Concert 2013 in Portugal. JP was at the event and had the big pleasure to meet Roberto for a little update chat backstage. Listen to this interview and also Roberto's latest single release on this week's edition of Radio International.     You can watch this interview also on our YouTube Channel - click here

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History:  A new quarter has begun which also marks the start of a new series called Denmark's Eurovision Song Contest History since Denmark for the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 and with this will be hosting the 2014 edition of the contest in Copenhagen the Team of Radio International thought to continue the tradition to reflect on all the Danish Eurovision entries over the years. Denmark has won the Eurovision Song Contest three time. It was first in 1963 when Grethe and Jorgen Ingmann performed "Dansevise", then the Olson Brothers won in 2000 with the song "Fly on the wings of love" and then in 2013 with Emmelie de Forest and "Only Teardrops". This week Dermot Manning continuous the brand new series looking back at the Eurovision entries from Denmark. 
 
Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight XL - The Semi Final entries that should have qualified for the Grand Final:  Returning to the airwaves this week and that in a longer version than the usual slot is Marcus Keppel-Palmer who will be joining JP for the entire third hour of this week's edition of the show to take a look at some of the great Eurovision entries that actually should have qualified for the Grand Finalof the Eurovision Song Contest. Due to the success of his feature and listeners request Nikki Allen from the United Kingdom selected what she thinks should have qualified for the Grand Final. Marcus and JP chat about those entries and of course also play'em. :) If you would like to submit your choice of Semi Final entries that should have made the Grand Final please send your list of 12 songs to CONTACT and let us know why you select each song.
