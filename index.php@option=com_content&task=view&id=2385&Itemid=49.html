Interview with D'Nash (Spain 2007): The Annual Eurovision Summer Concert took place in Stockholm, Sweden on Thursday 04 August 2011. 34 acts were part of this fantastic event which was a mix of Swedish and Eurovision performers from other countries. Eric Lehmann and JP from Radio International were present and interviewed some of the artists such as Spain's successful boyband D'Nash who represented Spain at the 2007 Eurovision Song Contest with the song "I love you mi vida". At that time D'Nash were four. Ony left the group after their Eurovision performance to go solo. "I love you me vida" reached Number 20 in Helsinki with 43 points. JP and Eric met Mikel, Basty & Javi for an interview after their performance in the event.
 
Interview with Siw Malmkvist (Sweden 1960 & Germany 1969): At the above mentioned event Radio International's JP and Eric were deeply honoured to have the chance of interviewing one of Sweden's and Germany's huge and still very popular artists - Siw Malmkvist who represented Sweden at the Eurovision Song Contest in 1960 with the song "Alla andra får varann" reaching Number 10. Then also being a big singer in Germany in the 60s Siw also represented Germany in 1969 with the song "Primaballerina" reaching a joint 9th with the entry from Sweden. Enjoy the interview with this charming singer. 
 
Interview with Lotta Engberg (Sweden 1987):  Another one of Sweden's great artists that stood on the Eurovision stage for her native country Sweden but also appeared in the Eurovision Concert on Stockholm last Thursday. Eric and JP interviewed Lotta Engberg after her performance during the event. Lotta represented Sweden in the 1987 Eurovision Song Contest with the song called "Boogaloo" coming 12th.

Eurovision Song Contest Spotlight - Songs that make your heart go BOOM:  Marcus Keppel-Palmer returns this week to the airwaves of Radio International filling in for Alasdair Rendall who returns in October. Marcus this week selected some songs from the Eurovision Song Contest that contains  the word "Boom". Let's see what Marcus has found. 
 
Home Composed Song Contest 2011: Radio International is delighted to announce the continuoued collaboration with the organisers of the Home Composed Song Contest 2011. Last year's event was won by the United Kingdom which like at the Eurovision Song Contest means that the winning country will virtually host the following year's contest. Andy Brooks is the prime organiser of the event and has launched the website which you can visit here and see the further details and may the best home composed song win. :)
